Learn the Dataset Features

This analyzer uses features derived from the PaySim synthetic dataset. Below is an easy-to-understand explanation of every input field so you can learn how financial transactions are recorded for fraud detection.

Step (Time)

The time step of the transaction. In this simulation, 1 step equals exactly 1 hour of real-world time. The entire dataset spans 744 steps, which perfectly represents exactly 31 days (one full month) of simulated financial transactions.

Transaction Type

The specific method used to move money. This can be one of five categories: CASH-IN (depositing money), CASH-OUT (withdrawing cash), DEBIT (using a card), PAYMENT (paying a bill), or TRANSFER. Note: Fraudulent activities are overwhelmingly found within the TRANSFER and CASH-OUT types.

Amount

The exact monetary value of the transaction in the local currency. Fraudulent transactions often involve unusually large amounts, or amounts that perfectly match the victim's total account balance to completely drain it in one go (known as an 'account sweep').

Sender's Balances (Origin)

  • Balance Before (oldbalanceOrg): The starting balance of the sender's account immediately before this specific transaction took place.
  • Balance After (newbalanceOrig): The updated balance of the sender's account right after the transaction is completed. For a normal transfer, this should perfectly equal the old balance minus the transaction amount.

Receiver's Balances (Destination)

  • Balance Before (oldbalanceDest): The starting balance of the receiver's account immediately before they received the money.
  • Balance After (newbalanceDest): The updated balance of the receiver's account right after the transaction is completed. For a normal transfer, this should perfectly equal the old balance plus the transaction amount.
